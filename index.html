The Tail End

Few of us stop to think about how we’re spending our time relative to the amount of time we have left to live. In a blog post titled The Tail End, writer and speaker Tim Urban explores different methods of visualizing a human lifespan to help us push ourselves to prioritize and make time for the things that truly matter to us. Drawing inspiration from Tim Urban’s article, we hope this interactive visualization inspires you to be more intentional with your time.

What if you grew up in a different country? The environment that we grow up in has a large impact on our life expectancy. The life expectancy from part 1 is only based on the average situation for someone of your gender and in your country. Life is fragile - it’s important to remember that there are so many different socioeconomic factors that could shorten or lengthen the amount of time we have left to do the things that matter to us!

Part 3:

Rather than units of time, what if we represented life in terms of activities or events? Does that encourage us to be even more mindful of who we’re spending our time? If you read one book every month, how many more books can you read before your time is up? If you visit your relatives once every two years, how many more times will you get to see them, ever?
